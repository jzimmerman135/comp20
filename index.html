A Cheesesteak For The Locals
Zim's Famous Cheesesteaks was founded in 1902 by Max Zimmerman, and has stayed within the Zim family name for 14 generations. Our cheesesteaks, made with delectable scrapple offcuts and locally sourced mystery-meat, has nourished Philadelphia through our highest highs—like the day the Eagles won the superbowl, to our lowest lows—like the morning after the Eagles won the superbowl.

Back when our city was known as "The Workshop of the World", Zim's Cheesesteaks opened to support our scant labor force by condensing eight meals worth of calories into a single sandwich, enabling textile workers to only need a lunch break once in four days.

As Zim's collides into the modern era, we remain vigilant in our daily pursuit of greatness. In a recent achievement, we crested the tremendous challenge of cleaning the bathroom for the first time since the Second World War. May our bathroom's next 78 years be as resilient as its last.
